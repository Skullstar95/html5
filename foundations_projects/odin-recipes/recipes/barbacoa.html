Barbacoa

Barbacoa is a traditional Mexican dish that involves slow-cooking meat, usually beef, lamb, or goat, in a pit or an oven. The meat is seasoned with various spices and herbs, resulting in tender, flavorful shredded meat that can be served in tacos, burritos, or on its own.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 lbs beef chuck roast or lamb shoulder
  • 2-3 dried guajillo chilies
  • 2-3 dried ancho chilies
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup beef or chicken broth
  • 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Soak the dried chilies in hot water for about 15 minutes until they are softened. Remove the seeds and stems, then blend the chilies with a little water to create a smooth paste.
  2. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat some oil over medium heat. Sear the meat on all sides until browned.
  3. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic to the pot and sauté until softened.
  4. Stir in the chili paste, cumin, oregano, apple cider vinegar, and broth. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Cover the pot and simmer on low heat for 3-4 hours, or until the meat is tender and easily shredded.
  6. Once cooked, remove the meat from the pot and shred it using two forks. Return the shredded meat to the pot and mix it with the sauce.
  7. Serve the barbacoa in tacos, burritos, or on its own with your favorite sides.
